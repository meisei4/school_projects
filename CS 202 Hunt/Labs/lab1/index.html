Grandma's Applie Pie

Memories

One of the best things I remember about my grandmother is her delicious applie pies. I remember cool Autumn evenings eating dessert on her porch in the center of Iowa and recall that well-known saying from Jane Austen that Good apple pies are a considerable part of our domestic happiness . To increase your domestice happiness, I've copied her recipe below, although there are some steps that I have since forgotten

Ingredients

  • 1 pastry for a 9 inch double crust
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 8 granny smith apples
    • peeled
    • cored
    • sliced

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 425 Degrees F. while the oven is heating
    1. Melt the butter in a saucepan.
    2. Stir in flower
    3. Add water and sugar
    4. Bring to boil
    5. Reduce temperature and let simmer
  2. Place the bottom crust in the pan and fill with apples. Cover with a lattice of crust
  3. Pour the sugar mixture over the crust
  4. Bake for 15 minutes
    1. Reduce the tempurature to 350 degrees.
    2. Continue baking for 34 to 40 minutes until the apples are soft
